Our Solar System

Our solar system consists of the sun and everything that revolves around it. This includes eight planets, many moons, asteroids, comets and small pieces of rock called meteoroids. The sun holds all these celestial bodies together with its strong gravitational pull. Between Mars and Jupiter, for example, there are many asteroids in a belt. There are also many small celestial bodies behind the planet Neptune. Our solar system is located in the Milky Way, which is a large collection of stars. It is around 4.6 billion years old and was formed from a huge cloud of gas and dust.

Sun

The Sun is the massive star at the center of our solar system. It provides energy and gravity to keep all planets in orbit.

Key Facts:

  • Type: Yellow dwarf (G2V)
  • Diameter: 1.39 million km
  • Surface Temp: ~5,500°C
  • Core Temp: ~15 million°C
  • Mass: 99.86% of solar system

The Planets

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Orbit: 88 Earth days
  • Day: 59 Earth days
  • Moons: 0
  • Surface Temp: -173°C to 427°C

Venus

Similar in size to Earth, Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets with a thick atmosphere trapping heat.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Orbit: 225 Earth days
  • Day: 243 Earth days
  • Moons: 0
  • Surface Temp: 462°C

Earth

Our home planet is the only place we know of so far that's inhabited by living things, with liquid water on the surface.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Orbit: 365.24 days
  • Day: 23.9 hours
  • Moons: 1
  • Surface Temp: -88°C to 58°C

Mars

The Red Planet is a dusty, cold desert world with a very thin atmosphere and evidence of ancient floods.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Orbit: 687 Earth days
  • Day: 24.6 hours
  • Moons: 2
  • Surface Temp: -153°C to 20°C

Jupiter

Twice as massive as all other planets combined, Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a gigantic storm bigger than Earth.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Orbit: 4,333 Earth days
  • Day: 9.9 hours
  • Moons: 95
  • Cloud Top Temp: -145°C

Saturn

Adorned with a dazzling system of icy rings, Saturn is unique among the planets with thousands of beautiful ringlets.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Orbit: 10,759 Earth days
  • Day: 10.7 hours
  • Moons: 146
  • Cloud Top Temp: -178°C

Uranus

Uranus rotates at a nearly 90-degree angle from the plane of its orbit, appearing to rotate on its side.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Orbit: 30,687 Earth days
  • Day: 17.2 hours
  • Moons: 27
  • Cloud Top Temp: -224°C

Neptune

Dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds, Neptune is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the naked eye.

Key Facts:

  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Orbit: 60,190 Earth days
  • Day: 16.1 hours
  • Moons: 14
  • Cloud Top Temp: -214°C
